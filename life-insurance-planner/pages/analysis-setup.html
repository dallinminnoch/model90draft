! Why this?

Protection Modeling Inputs capture the client's raw financial facts. Assumption controls define how those facts are interpreted for planning, including default assumptions, tax treatment, liquidity haircuts, and recommendation guardrails. These assumptions matter for life insurance because they help determine how much existing wealth can realistically support survivors, reducing the risk of recommending unnecessary coverage while still protecting against underfunded needs.

Survivor & Support Assumptions

PMI captures current spouse income facts. These assumptions control how survivor income is treated after death. Balanced assumes the survivor continues working with a temporary 25% reduction and a 3-month income delay.

Default Settings:

Survivor income treatment

Survivor income treatment field help ?
  • Apply survivor income offset: lets Needs count survivor income against the support gap.
  • Apply survivor income start delay: delays when survivor income begins reducing support need.
  • Apply survivor income growth: saves a future-use growth assumption; current Needs does not project it.
  • Maximum reliance: caps how much support can rely on survivor income.

Survivor scenario

Survivor scenario field help ?
  • Survivor continues working: sets whether survivor income should be considered after death.
  • Expected work reduction: reduces survivor income for caregiving, transition, or schedule changes.
  • Start delay: sets how many months pass before survivor income is applied.
  • Income growth: saves a future-use survivor income growth rate.
  • Income horizon: sets how long survivor income remains available for planning.
